The five user roles of caAERS provide access to different tabs of the application. When your account is created, you can be assigned one or more user roles, depending on your responsibilities. The roles and their responsibilities are:
 

  • System Administrator (super user) &endash; Responsible for maintaining the caAERS hardware and software; has access to all tabs in caAERS; resolves user issues
  • Site Coordinator &endash; Responsible for maintaining information about the site
  • Study Coordinator &endash; Responsible for setting up the study in the system, creating the protocols, defining adverse events, and setting the general parameters of a study
  • Adverse Event Coordinator &endash; Responsible for entering adverse events as they are defined by the study or sponsor
  • Subject Coordinator &endash; Responsible for adding subjects and may also report adverse events
 

In addition to the user roles that allow access to caAERS, there are two other roles that do not have access to caAERS but are necessary for the proper use of caAERS. These user roles are:

  • Study Subject &endash; a qualified patient enrolled in a study
  • Investigator &endash; person or organization who is leading a study
 

Note: If an investigator wants to/needs to access caAERS, an account can be created for him/her. However, investigators are not required to have caAERS access.
